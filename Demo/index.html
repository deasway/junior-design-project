Space Dinosaur Project

In a recent interview Georgia Tech's Ayanna Howard credits The Bionic Woman as inspiring her initial forays into the world of robotics. iRobot founder and former head of the MIT AI Laboratory, Rodney Brooks, credits 2001 A Space Odyssey for sparking his interest in machine learning. Fang Zhang's prize-winning work on CRISPR was born out of a fascination with Jurassic Park as a child. There is no doubt that science fiction has inspired some of the brightest minds of the twenty-first century, and there is evidence linking the relationship between science fiction and STEM littered across biographies, interviews, and articles all over the internet.

This project will seek to use web scraping to parse various science databases for references to science fiction works, concepts, and authors that have influenced modern technologies. This archive will then be used to produce statistical models that demonstrate how the world of speculative science fiction and modern scientific thought are hardwired at the most basic levels.
